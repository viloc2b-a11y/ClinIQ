# ClinIQ Value Narrative: Budget Execution & Post-Award Revenue Protection

Use this for **website copy**, **deck intros**, **sponsor conversations**, and **internal alignment**. It is aligned to ClinIQ’s direction: a **budget execution and revenue protection layer** for research sites—not a replacement for every finance system, and not “budget software” alone.

---

## Positioning (one paragraph)

**ClinIQ is a post-award financial truth system for clinical research sites.** It connects what the study **actually did** under the budget to what should be **invoiced**, what **cash** applied, what is still **open**, and what needs **attention**—with deterministic rules so finance, operations, and sponsors can trust the picture after startup, not only at award.

---

## Core problem

Clinical trials run on **contracts and budgets**, but day-to-day reality is **execution**: visits shift, support costs accumulate, and sponsor payment behavior diverges from the spreadsheet model.

Sites lose money in the gap between:

- **“We did the work.”**
- **“We invoiced correctly.”**
- **“We collected and reconciled what we were owed.”**

That gap widens after **first patient in**—when nobody has time to rebuild the model in Excel every week.

---

## Why spreadsheets fail (for this layer)

Spreadsheets are flexible; they are also **private, fragile, and non-auditable** at scale.

- **Version drift:** “Budget v4_final_REALLY.xlsx” vs. what finance actually paid.
- **No stable lineage:** Hard to prove a line item on an invoice maps to a visit, fee, or exception rule.
- **No cash truth:** Open AR becomes a tab, not a system—aging and short pays hide until quarter close.
- **No operational queue:** “Who owns sponsor X?” is a meeting, not a ranked list.

Spreadsheets are fine for **negotiation math**; they are a weak **system of record** for post-award revenue.

---

## Why sites lose revenue after startup

Common patterns:

- **Partial and short pays** sit on accounts without a clear policy (dispute vs. accept vs. rebill).
- **Overdue invoices** lack a single prioritized view tied to **dollars at risk**.
- **Write-offs** happen in the GL without a clean link back to **which study / sponsor / invoice** drove the loss.
- **Execution changes** (actual visits, pass-throughs, out-of-window activity) never flow back to **invoice-ready truth**.

Revenue protection is not only “negotiate a better per-visit rate.” It is **closing the loop after award** so earned revenue does not evaporate in operations.

---

## How ClinIQ closes the gap

| Capability | What it gives the site |
|------------|-------------------------|
| **Execution → invoice-ready structure** | A path from what happened to claim / invoice packages with traceable lines—not a one-off export. |
| **Posted invoice & cash application** | Stable invoice ids, payments, allocations, write-offs, open balances, aging—explicit rules, repeatable outputs. |
| **Risk visibility** | High / medium / low framing with reasons (overdue, short-pay, aging, stale partial pay)—for prioritization, not guesswork. |
| **Collections action queue** | Recommended next actions and a **priority rank** so work is ordered. |
| **Command summary** | One snapshot for leadership: outstanding AR, risk-tier dollars, overdue / short-pay counts, and top priority invoices. |

ClinIQ does **not** need to replace your ERP to matter. It needs to be the **thin, honest layer** that answers: *what we earned, what’s open, what’s at risk, and what to do next.*

---

## What ClinIQ is not (credible boundaries)

- **Not** a replacement for your general ledger or bank reconciliation.
- **Not** automated legal collections or dunning—it **surfaces** action; people decide.
- **Not** probabilistic “AI matching” for cash in v1—determinism is a feature for audits and demos.

Stating boundaries builds trust with sponsors and CFOs.

## Key product language (copy-ready)

**Short tagline options**

- *Post-award revenue protection for clinical research sites.*
- *From execution to open AR—one deterministic financial thread.*
- *Budget execution that does not end at award.*

**Elevator (30 seconds)**

ClinIQ helps research sites protect revenue after startup by connecting execution and billing to posted invoices, cash, and open balances—then prioritizing what needs follow-up. It is deterministic and traceable, so finance and operations share one truth for what is owed, what is at risk, and what to do next.

**For sponsors (trust-building)**

We are not asking sponsors to adopt a new payment rail. We are giving sites a **clear, consistent picture** of obligation and application so conversations about partial pays, disputes, and aging start from **shared facts**.

**For sites (internal)**

Stop treating open AR as a spreadsheet tab. Treat it as a **ranked operational object** tied to studies and invoices you can defend.

## Wording choices to revisit later

- **“Revenue protection”** vs. **“revenue integrity”** — both resonate; compliance-heavy audiences may prefer *integrity*.
- **“Command summary”** is engineering-accurate; externally you might say **“AR snapshot”** or **“collections dashboard feed.”**
- **Module 5 / Module 6** — useful internally; customer-facing copy should use **claims & invoice package** and **posted invoice & cash** instead of module numbers.
